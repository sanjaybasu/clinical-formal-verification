# Supplementary appendix

Supplementary material for "Which decisions live in the provable layer? An assurance-gap
framework and a benchmark for formal verification of clinical AI guardrails." All artifacts
referenced here are in the public repository; every number derives from the committed code and
outputs.

## Appendix S1. Benchmark schema and construction

Each item validates against `benchmark/schema/benchmark.schema.json`; ground truth validates
against `benchmark/schema/answer_key.schema.json`. Variables are boolean, ordered enum, or
bounded integer, which keeps every input space finite. Rule sets are decision rule sets (a total
function under priority or first-match conflict resolution, with a default block) or finite
transition systems (events with finite-domain parameters, guards, updates, and emitted actions).
Properties are invariant, reachability, mutual exclusion, monotonicity (a two-copy template over
the inputs and a perturbed copy), and temporal (a linear-temporal formula over a transition
system). The grammar and semantics are documented in `benchmark/schema/README.md`; the deterministic
generators are `benchmark/generate.py`, `generate_hard.py`, and `generate_compute.py`.

Ground truth is established without a solver: exhaustive enumeration for decision rule sets and
breadth-first reachability to a bound exceeding the diameter of the finite state for transition
systems. A violated item's witness is confirmed by concrete execution (`verifier/replay.py`),
which is what makes the benchmark non-circular for scoring verifiers.

## Appendix S5. Verification encodings

Decision rule sets compile to one output term per output variable, a nested conditional over the
rules in resolution order, with the default as the innermost branch. An invariant is checked as the
unsatisfiability of its negation; reachability as the unsatisfiability of the forbidden pattern;
mutual exclusion as the unsatisfiability of any listed pair being jointly asserted; monotonicity by
a two-copy encoding constraining all non-perturbed inputs equal and asserting the monotone output
moves the wrong way. Transition systems are checked by bounded model checking, unrolling the
transition relation (a disjunction over enabled transitions and a stutter, selected by an index
variable) to the bound and asserting a reachable state violates the property; a k-induction step is
attempted for an unbounded result and is time-boxed to five seconds. A solver result of unknown is
reported as an abstention, never as a proof. Source: `verifier/`.

## Appendix S7. Reproducibility and protocol

The evaluation protocol, fixed before any baseline ran, is `experiments/protocol.md`; the
environment and method availability are `experiments/environment.md`; and every departure from the
protocol, including the partially-refuted pre-registered hypothesis, the two exploratory suites,
the decoding caveat for the language-model judge, and the verifier unsoundness bug found and fixed
during the evaluation, is recorded in `experiments/deviations.md`. The benchmark validator is
`benchmark/validate.py` and the verifier self-tests are in `tests/` (15 of 15 pass).
