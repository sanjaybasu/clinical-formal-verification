# Target venue and submission requirements

Target venue: AI (MDPI), ISSN 2673-2688, indexed, impact factor 5.0. Special Issue "Agentic AI
for Healthcare: Reasoning, Safety, and Clinically Aligned Autonomous Systems." Guest editors:
Abdul Rehman, Faisal Saeed, Muhammad Mazhar Rathore. Submission deadline 31 March 2027; rolling
publication on acceptance. APC 1800 CHF. Open access, CC-BY-4.0. Editorial office ai@mdpi.com;
a short abstract or tentative title may be sent ahead.

This file is the formatting and compliance checklist that the manuscript and submission package
follow. It is drawn from the journal Instructions for Authors.

## Article type

Article (original research): Abstract, Keywords, Introduction, Materials and Methods, Results,
Discussion, Conclusions (optional), then back matter. This paper reports a benchmark plus
experiments, so it is an Article, not a Review.

## Front matter

- Title: concise, specific; no running head; identify the study type where relevant.
- Author list with full first and last names; PubMed/MEDLINE affiliation format (full address,
  city, zip, state/province, country); at least one corresponding author; author emails are
  published. Generative AI tools cannot be authors.
- Abstract: about 200 words, single paragraph, structured content without headings, in the
  order background, methods, results, conclusion; no result that is not in the main text; no
  exaggeration.
- Keywords: three to ten, specific to the article.

## Body

- Introduction comprehensible to scientists outside the topic; state purpose, significance,
  hypotheses; review the field and cite key publications.
- Materials and Methods in enough detail to replicate; name and version of all software; state
  whether code is available; include any preregistration codes. Disclose how generative AI was
  used to generate text, data, graphics, or to assist design, collection, analysis, or
  interpretation. Superficial editing (grammar, spelling, formatting) need not be declared.
- Results: concise and precise.
- Discussion: interpret against prior work and the hypotheses; state limitations; future
  directions. May be combined with Results.
- Conclusions: optional.

## Citations and references (differs from the author's usual superscript style)

- In-text references are bracketed numbers in order of appearance: [1], [1-3], [1,3]; placed
  before punctuation. With pagination: [5] (p. 10) or [6] (pp. 101-105). This journal does not
  use superscript citation markers; the manuscript uses bracketed numbers.
- Reference list in ACS style, numbered in order of appearance, full titles. DOIs encouraged.
- Citations in supplementary files must also appear in the main text and reference list.

## Back matter required for an Article

- Supplementary Materials (named Figure S1, Table S1, etc.).
- Author Contributions (CRediT taxonomy).
- Funding: state explicitly; per the author's default, "This research received no external
  funding."
- Institutional Review Board Statement: resolved. WCG IRB approved these observational studies
  of retrospective data that do not affect participant treatment decisions, with a waiver of
  informed consent given the use of de-identified data, under IRB Tracking ID 20253751. The
  manuscript carries this statement. The public benchmark is synthetic or public-source derived
  and involves no human subjects; the private COMPASS-derived split is retrospective and
  de-identified and is covered by this approval.
- Informed Consent Statement: as applicable; likely a waiver or not applicable given no
  individual-level human data are presented.
- Data Availability Statement: required. Planned statement: the benchmark, verifier, baselines,
  and analysis code are openly available on GitHub and archived with a DOI; the private split
  is not released due to data-use restrictions and is reported in aggregate.
- Acknowledgments, including the generative-AI use statement in MDPI's prescribed wording if AI
  tools were used to prepare text, data, or graphics.
- Conflicts of Interest: the standard Waymark wording, that the authors are employees of
  Waymark, a public benefit organization that provides free social and medical services for
  patients receiving Medicaid.

## Reporting guideline

The plan calls for TRIPOD-AI adapted or a justified alternative. This study is not a prediction
model development or validation study, so TRIPOD-AI does not map cleanly. The verification study
reports a benchmark and a head-to-head method comparison; the manuscript justifies its reporting
approach (a structured benchmark-and-comparison checklist) in Materials and Methods and supplies
a completed checklist as supplementary material. Resolve the exact guideline choice in Milestone
5.

## Cover letter

Required, concise, explains significance and fit to the journal scope. Must include the two
statements: that the manuscript is not under consideration or published elsewhere, and that all
authors approved submission. Reviewer suggestions go in the submission system, not the cover
letter. Do not head the letter with "Cover Letter" or "Date:"; begin with the date itself.

## Open formatting notes

- Free format is accepted at first submission; format to the template at revision.
- Equations must be editable (Word equation editor or MathType, or LaTeX), not images.
- Figures at 600 dpi or higher, PNG/JPEG/TIFF, supplied in a single zip.
- SI units. Acronyms defined on first use in each of abstract, main text, first figure or table.
